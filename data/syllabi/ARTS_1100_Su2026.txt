COURSE DESCRIPTION
Survey and theory of art throughout the world and throughout history, focusing on the analysis of art forms, technical procedures, subject matter, composition, theory, art philosophy and cultural and social values and influences. Two-dimensional and three-dimensional arts and the formal media theories of visual arts will be examined via lectures, projects, discussions and presentations.

CORE IMPACTS DOMAIN: Arts, Humanities and Ethics
Orienting Question: How do I interpret the human experience through creative, linguistic, and philosophical works?
Learning Outcome: Students will effectively analyze and interpret the meaning, cultural significance, and ethical implications of literary/philosophical texts in English or other languages, or of works in the visual/performing arts.
Career-Ready Competencies: Ethical Reasoning, Information Literacy, Intercultural Competence

COURSE GOALS AND OBJECTIVES
1. Understand art as critical to cultural and global awareness.
2. Acquire a basic visual literacy.
3. Gain knowledge of creative works from multiple cultures and time periods.
4. Become aware of art and cultural activity locally and globally.

IEE Goals met:
- IEE-1: Clearly communicate ideas in written and oral form.
- IEE-2: Demonstrate creativity and critical thinking in inter- and multi-disciplinary contexts.
- IEE-4: Develop intercultural awareness of diverse viewpoints and of local and global perspectives.
- IEE-5: Produce scholarly or creative works that reflect information literacy knowledge, skills, and dispositions.

REQUIRED COURSE MATERIALS
No traditional textbook is used for this course. Readings and videos are attached on Brightspace (Desire to Learn / D2L).

GRADING
- Notes: 20%
- Exams: 15% (2 open-note, timed, multiple-choice exams)
- Studio Activities: 35% (hands-on activities: Texture Rubbing, Blind Contour Drawing, Post Modern Sculpture, Logo Redesign)
- Discussion Board: 30%

ASSESSMENT TOOLS
- Discussion Boards: Based on recorded lectures and process concept videos on artists
- Studio Activities: Hands-on techniques covering visual art concepts
- Notes: Weekly handwritten notes on designated chapters
- Exams: Midterm and final, multiple-choice, open-note, timed

KEY TOPICS COVERED
- What is Art / Purposes of Art
- Visual Elements (line, shape, color, texture, space, form, value)
- Principles of Design (balance, emphasis, movement, pattern, rhythm, unity)
- Drawing techniques (blind contour drawing)
- Painting
- Printmaking
- Sculpture and Craft (Post-Modern Sculpture)
- Photography
- Film and Performance Art
- Graphic Arts (Logo Design)
- Art history: world art, multiple cultures and time periods
- Art philosophy and theory
- Cultural and social values in art
- 2D and 3D arts and formal media theories of visual arts
